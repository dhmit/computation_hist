Floating Point Numbers

Computers such as the IBM-704 store real numbers in a format called floating-point. The floating point format allows computers to store and perform arithmetic upon numbers of diverse magnitudes with appreciable precision. A floating point system is defined by two numbers: the Base and the Precision (The base is sometimes called the radix).

A real number represented as a floating point itself has two parts: a Significand and an Exponent.
